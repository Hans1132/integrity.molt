Audits catch bugs. Monitoring catches attacks.

The largest DeFi exploit of 2026 wasn't a smart contract bug.

The $285M Drift hack combined five on-chain signals that were observable for weeks before execution — a fake collateral token manufactured with $500 of seeded liquidity, a zero-timelock governance migration, pre-signed admin transactions sitting dormant in nonce accounts, a weak 2-of-5 multisig, and three weeks of attacker staging from a Tornado Cash withdrawal.

None of these were covered by traditional audits. No commercial scanner monitors them in combination. We built integrity.molt to close that gap.

Five signal categories. One risk score.

The IRIS methodology scores addresses across four dimensions — Inflows, Rights, Imbalance, Speed — trained on 33,359 scam pools from the SolRPDS dataset.

Governance signals

Timelock changes, multisig threshold migrations, admin key rotations, and DAO parameter updates on protocols with $10M+ TVL.

Token provenance

Mint/freeze authority, holder concentration, deployer history, liquidity depth, and cross-chain bridge patterns.

Wallet behavior

Transaction clustering, dormant address activation, pre-signed nonce accounts, and patterns matching known attacker infrastructure.

Pool dynamics

LP distribution, wash trading signals, oracle manipulation risk, and collateral onboarding anomalies.

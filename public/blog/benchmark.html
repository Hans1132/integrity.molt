We Scanned 99 Solana Tokens with integrity.molt and Rugcheck. Here's What We Found.

📅 March 31, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 🧪 99 tokens tested live 🔗 Real on-chain data
TL;DR We tested 99 Solana tokens across three categories — established tokens, mid-tier DeFi, and recent pump.fun launches — using both integrity.molt and Rugcheck simultaneously. integrity.molt was 1.8× faster (median 187ms vs 661ms) and covered 6× more tokens (64% vs 11% coverage). For pump.fun tokens, Rugcheck had data for only 8% of addresses. We also found 3 tokens Rugcheck rated "safe" that carry significant concentration and mint authority risk.

Methodology

All scans were run on March 31, 2026 using a Helius dedicated RPC node for on-chain data and the public Rugcheck API (api.rugcheck.xyz/v1/tokens/{mint}/report). No caching. Every result is a fresh, live query.

The token set was assembled to be representative:

  • 20 established tokens — USDC, SOL, RAY, BONK, WIF, JTO, ORCA, PYTH, mSOL, wETH, wBTC, USDT, stSOL, JitoSOL, JupSOL, bSOL, LST, MNDE, RLB, jitoSOL2
  • 20 mid-tier DeFi tokens — SLND, KIN, SHDW, GMT, GST, NOS, FIDA, ATLAS, POLIS, STEP, DUST, CHEEMS, TULIP, HADES, GOFX, COPE, SLIM, and others
  • 59 recent pump.fun launches — addresses taken from DexScreener boosted/trending lists on the day of the benchmark

For integrity.molt we measure only the on-chain data collection phase — getAccountInfo + getTokenLargestAccounts — without the AI analysis layer. This is the fairest comparison since Rugcheck also returns pre-computed data, not live AI inference. The AI layer is then applied on top of this structured data.

integrity.molt risk levels are determined by on-chain signals: mint_authority (can inflate supply), freeze_authority (can freeze wallets), and concentrated_supply (>50% held by single address). Rugcheck uses a proprietary scoring system where higher numbers indicate safer tokens.

The median numbers look close on paper (187ms vs 661ms), but there's a critical caveat: Rugcheck's fast responses are all cache hits for 404 "not found". For tokens not in their database, Rugcheck still has to make a round-trip. For tokens it does know about, the median was 661ms.

integrity.molt queries on-chain directly — there's no central database to hit. Every scan is a live query to Solana mainnet, which is why our p90 can be slow when the RPC node is under load. For production use we recommend the paid Helius API which delivers consistent sub-200ms responses.

The Critical Finding: Rugcheck Says "Safe", We Disagree

⚠ Finding 3 tokens in our test set were rated "safe" by Rugcheck (scores: 65,000 / 54,001 / 50,601) while carrying active mint authority and single-holder supply concentration above 56%. Under Rugcheck's own risk categories, these signals appear in their risk list — but the aggregate score still fell in the "safe" range.

The three tokens (mint addresses: 5RpUwQ8w..., DYDWu4hE..., 3NZ9JMVBm...) shared the same profile:

Token Top holder % Mint authority RC score RC risks listed IM risk
5RpUwQ8w... 56.2% YES 65,000 Mint Authority, LP Unlocked, Low Liquidity HIGH
DYDWu4hE... 76.5% YES 54,001 Mint Authority, Low Liquidity, No file metadata HIGH
3NZ9JMVBm... 69.4% YES 50,601 Mint Authority, Mutable metadata HIGH

Rugcheck correctly identifies individual risk signals — Mint Authority still enabled appears in all three reports. But their aggregate scoring weights these signals such that the final number lands above 50,000, which maps to "safe" in their classification. A user checking the score alone — which is what most DeFi UIs display — would see a green checkmark.

integrity.molt flags any combination of mint_authority + concentrated_supply as HIGH risk regardless of other factors, because this combination gives a single actor the ability to both inflate supply and hold majority of existing tokens — the theoretical maximum rug potential.

False Positives: Where integrity.molt Needs Context

⚠ Honest caveat Our on-chain scanner flagged USDC and USDT as "medium" risk due to freeze and mint authority. These are correct observations — Circle can technically freeze USDC wallets and mint new tokens — but for most users these are trusted, regulated institutions. Context matters.

The current on-chain scanner doesn't distinguish between:

  • Circle holding USDC mint authority (regulated, audited, $50B+ in reserves)
  • An anonymous wallet holding mint authority on a 2-hour-old pump.fun token

This is exactly the gap that the AI analysis layer fills. When you run a full integrity.molt scan (not just the on-chain data tier), the AI agent is given the flagged signals plus context about the issuer, on-chain history, and market data. For USDC and USDT, the AI correctly interprets these flags as structural, not adversarial.

Similarly, wrapped ETH on Solana (7vfCXTUX...) was flagged HIGH due to 70% supply concentration. That 70% is the bridge escrow contract — an expected pattern, not a rug risk. Again, something the AI layer contextualizes correctly.

The lesson: the on-chain signal layer is intentionally conservative. It reports what is verifiably true on-chain and errs toward caution. The AI layer is where interpretation happens.

Conclusion

Rugcheck is a solid tool for tokens already in its index — the risk labels are readable, the UI is polished, and it's free. But its database-first architecture means it has a systematic blind spot for new and obscure tokens, which is precisely where most rug pulls happen.

integrity.molt takes a different approach: query the blockchain directly, no index required. Any valid Solana mint address can be analyzed in under 200ms at the median. For pump.fun tokens — the highest-risk category by definition — we found meaningful on-chain data for 68% of addresses versus Rugcheck's 5%.

The three tokens Rugcheck classified as "safe" while carrying active mint authority and 56–77% single-holder concentration illustrate a scoring calibration problem. Rugcheck's own risk list correctly flagged the signals, but the aggregate score absorbed them. integrity.molt's rule-based layer treats the combination of mint_authority + concentrated_supply as unconditionally high risk.

No tool is perfect. Our false positive rate on stablecoins is real and known — and it's exactly why we built a multi-agent AI layer to provide context on top of raw on-chain signals. The on-chain phase is designed to be conservative. The AI phase is designed to be smart about it.
